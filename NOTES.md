# NOTES.md

The final model combines gradient clipping (1.0), a learning rate of 6e-4, GPT-2 style scaled residual initialization, 192-dimensional embeddings, 6 attention heads, a context length of 160, and a Greedy Longest-Match UTF-8 tokenizer with byte-level fallback and a vocabulary size of 500. The final submission achieved a BPB of 2.0845, improving upon the baseline score of 2.3718 by approximately 12.11%. Tokenization improvements provided the largest performance gains by substantially reducing the effective token count while preserving lossless UTF-8 encoding. Optimization improvements, particularly gradient clipping and an increased learning rate, significantly improved convergence under the strict 2000 optimizer step budget. Larger embedding dimensions and architectural refinements further improved performance while remaining within the 2 million parameter constraint. Several unsuccessful experiments, including AdamW, cosine learning rate scheduling, and alternative parameter sharing strategies, were retained and analyzed to guide subsequent modifications. The training loss continued decreasing at step 2000 across multiple successful runs suggesting that the model had not fully converged at the end of training. This observation indicates that the primary bottleneck of the challenge was the optimization budget rather than model capacity limitations. Given additional optimization steps or training time, further improvements are likely possible through more expressive tokenization strategies and extended optimization. The final model was selected as it consistently provided the best trade-off between performance, parameter efficiency, and robustness across all experiments.
