Selected Final Submission Model

  • 4 Layer GPT-style Decoder Only Language Model implemented entirely in PyTorch.
  • 192 Dimensional Token Embeddings.
  • 6 Attention Heads.
  • Context Length = 160.
  • GPT-2 Style Scaled Residual Initialization.
  • Gradient Clipping = 1.0.
  • Learning Rate = 6e-4.
  • Adam Optimizer.
  • Greedy Longest Match UTF-8 Tokenizer.
  • Vocabulary Size = 500.
  • Lossless UTF-8 Encoding with Byte Level Fallback.

Implementation Details

  • Implemented entirely using pure PyTorch, NumPy and Python standard libraries.
  • No pretrained weights were used at any stage of experimentation.
  • The tokenizer was trained exclusively on the provided training corpus.
  • All experiments were performed entirely on CPU.
  • No external language modeling libraries or pretrained models were utilized.
  • The final model satisfies all parameter and optimizer step constraints specified in the challenge statement.

Successful Experiments

  • Gradient Clipping.
  • Increasing the Learning Rate.
  • GPT-2 Style Scaled Residual Initialization.

  • Increasing the Number of Attention Heads.

Unsuccessful Experiments

  • AdamW with Weight Decay.
  • Cosine Learning Rate Scheduling.
  • Aggressive Learning Rate Increase.
  • Weight Tying.
  • Large Context Length (256).
  • Alternative Vocabulary Allocation Strategies.

All unsuccessful experiments were retained and analyzed rather than discarded. Their outcomes significantly influenced subsequent design decisions and helped identify optimization and tokenization bottlenecks throughout development.

Key Observations

  • Tokenizer modifications produced substantially larger gains than individual architectural modifications.
  • Optimization improvements provided significant gains during the early stages of experimentation.
  • Training loss continued decreasing at step 2000 across multiple successful runs.
  • The final model does not appear to have completely converged under the available optimization budget.
  • The primary bottleneck of the challenge appears to be the strict optimizer step constraint rather than parameter count limitations.
  • The experimental results suggest that additional optimization steps would likely yield further improvements even without increasing model size.

Final Remarks

The final submission improves upon the baseline development score by approximately 12.11% while satisfying all optimization, parameter and implementation constraints of the challenge. Multiple successful experiments exhibited decreasing training loss at the final optimization step suggesting that the model had not completely converged under the available optimization budget. Both successful and unsuccessful experiments have been retained and analyzed throughout development, with tokenization improvements providing the largest gains among all explored modifications. The selected final submission model represents the strongest balance between performance, parameter efficiency, robustness and adherence to the challenge constraints.
